Customer Persona Components

Click each section to explore the key elements that make a comprehensive customer persona

Demographic Information
▼
Age range, gender, geographic location, income level, education, and occupation provide the foundational framework for understanding who the client is within a societal context.
Psychographic Characteristics
▼
Values, attitudes, interests, and lifestyle choices reveal why certain fitness approaches appeal to specific individuals. For example, a client who values efficiency and productivity may respond well to high-intensity, time-efficient training methods, whilst someone who values social connection may prefer group training environments.
Behavioural Patterns
▼
How potential clients currently engage with fitness, their media consumption habits, their purchasing behaviours, and their preferred communication channels inform how trainers should structure their social media presence and content delivery.
Goals and Motivations
▼
Understanding what clients hope to achieve through fitness training whether aesthetic changes, performance improvements, health management, or psychological benefits enables trainers to position their services as solutions to specific aspirations.
Pain Points and Barriers
▼
Identifying the obstacles that prevent potential clients from achieving their fitness goals allows trainers to address concerns proactively through content and service design. Common barriers include time constraints, previous negative experiences, financial limitations, physical limitations or injuries, and lack of knowledge or confidence.
