Tone Considerations for Fitness Content

Click each consideration to explore appropriate tone and language choices

Avoiding Shame-Based Messaging
▼
Fitness content should never employ guilt, shame, or fear as motivational tactics. Language such as "work off that weekend," "earn your meals," or "summer bodies are made in winter" reinforces harmful relationships with exercise and food.
❌ Avoid
"Work off that weekend" • "Earn your meals" • "Summer bodies are made in winter"
✓ Instead Use
"Build strength that improves your daily life" • "Fuel your training for better performance" • "Feel more energised and confident"
Inclusive and Body-Neutral Language
▼
Content should avoid language that assumes universal aesthetic goals or values certain body types over others. Terms such as "bikini body," "problem areas," or "trouble zones" alienate many potential clients and reinforce narrow beauty standards.
❌ Avoid
"Bikini body" • "Problem areas" • "Trouble zones" • Assumptions about universal aesthetic goals
✓ Instead Use
"Strengthen your posterior chain" • "Improve your movement quality" • "Increase your training capacity" • Focus on function, capability, and health
Empowering Rather than Prescriptive
▼
Tone should position clients as capable decision-makers rather than passive recipients of instructions. Language patterns should respect client autonomy rather than feeling restrictive and disempowering.
❌ Avoid
"You must" • "Never" • "Always" • Dictatorial language that feels restrictive
✓ Instead Use
"You can choose" • "Options include" • "Consider whether" • Language that respects autonomy
Balancing Motivation with Realism
▼
Whilst motivational content has value, overly hyperbolic language sets unrealistic expectations and can damage trust when reality fails to match promises. Effective tone balances encouragement with honesty about effort requirements and realistic timelines.
❌ Avoid
"Transform your life in 30 days" • "Achieve the body of your dreams" • Overly hyperbolic promises
✓ Instead Use
Balanced encouragement with honest effort requirements • Realistic timelines • Build trust through honesty
Professional Boundaries in Personal Sharing
▼
When sharing personal experiences, trainers should maintain appropriate boundaries between relatable honesty and oversharing that may make followers uncomfortable or undermine professional credibility.
✓ Appropriate Sharing
Discussing challenges with training consistency demonstrates relatability and builds connection with followers
❌ Oversharing
Extensive discussion of personal problems unrelated to fitness may be inappropriate for professional platforms and can undermine credibility
