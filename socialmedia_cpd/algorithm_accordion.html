Social Media Algorithm & Optimisation

Understanding what makes content perform and how to optimise your fitness content for social media platforms

1
Key Algorithm Factors
‚ñº

Whilst each platform employs proprietary algorithms, several factors consistently influence content performance across Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, and other major platforms.

üìä Engagement Rate
The proportion of viewers who interact with content relative to total reach

Higher engagement rates signal valuable content worthy of broader distribution.

Example

Post A: 200 likes from 1,000 viewers = 20% engagement rate (stronger algorithmic promotion)

Post B: 300 likes from 3,000 viewers = 10% engagement rate (weaker algorithmic promotion)

Post A will typically receive more algorithmic boost despite fewer total likes.

‚ñ∂Ô∏è Completion Rate
For video content, the percentage of viewers who watch until completion

Videos that retain viewer attention throughout receive preferential treatment compared to videos with high drop-off rates. This incentivises creating engaging content that maintains interest rather than lengthy content that viewers abandon.

üí¨ Meaningful Interactions
Substantive engagement beyond simple likes

Algorithms increasingly weight comments, shares, saves, and DMs more heavily than simple likes. Content that generates conversation receives more distribution than passive-like content.

High-Value Interactions (More Heavily Weighted)
  • Comments and replies
  • Shares with followers
  • Saves to collections
  • Direct messages
Lower-Value Interactions
  • Likes
  • Views without interaction
‚è∞ Timeliness and Recency
How fresh your content is relative to other content

Recent content receives distribution priority over older content. However, content generating strong engagement may receive renewed distribution ("second wave" reach) if engagement signals remain strong.

üîó Relationship Strength
Your connection with specific followers

Users see more content from accounts they frequently interact with. Building consistent engagement with followers increases the likelihood your content appears in their feeds regularly, creating a positive feedback loop.

üé¨ Content Format Preferences
Algorithmic favouritism toward specific content types

Platforms periodically adjust preferences for specific formats to promote new features. Currently, short-form video content (Reels, TikToks, Shorts) receives substantial algorithmic favour across most platforms.

Action: Monitor platform announcements and industry analysis to understand current format preferences.

Video Content Optimisation
‚ñº

Given current algorithmic preferences for video content, fitness professionals should prioritise video creation and optimisation.

üéØ Hook Viewers Immediately
Capture attention within first 1-3 seconds to prevent scrolling

Opening frames should immediately communicate value or create curiosity.

Strong Opening Hooks
  • "The mistake that's limiting your squat depth"
  • "Why your shoulder press feels difficult"
  • "Three exercises for stronger running"
  • "If you're doing this, you're wasting 30% of the exercise"
‚ú® Maintain Engagement Throughout
Keep viewers watching to completion
  • Use movement and dynamic camera angles
  • Include text overlays highlighting key teaching points
  • Incorporate b-roll footage providing visual variety
  • Apply dynamic editing that maintains interest without becoming chaotic
‚è±Ô∏è Optimise Video Length
Match length to platform preferences and content type
Optimal Video Lengths

Quick Tips: 7-15 seconds (Instagram Reels, TikTok)

Technique Demonstrations: 20-45 seconds

Longer Content (60+ seconds): Requires exceptional value to maintain engagement throughout

üìù Include Captions
Make content accessible to sound-off viewers

Many users watch videos without sound. Text overlays or burned-in captions make content accessible whilst increasing watch time from sound-off viewers.

üîÅ Encourage Replays
Create content worth watching more than once
  • Include multiple teaching points that benefit from re-watching
  • Use quick transitions that reveal information gradually
  • Make content information-dense so single viewings don't capture everything

Caption and Content Optimisation
‚ñº

Captions significantly impact engagement by providing context, encouraging interaction, and extending time spent on posts.

üìñ Write Engaging Captions
Longer, valuable captions keep users on posts longer

Write captions that encourage reading rather than minimal text. Longer captions signal value to algorithms, but should provide genuine substance rather than filler.

‚ùå Minimal Caption

"Love this exercise."

‚úì Engaging Caption

"The biggest squat mistake I see is knees collapsing inward. Here's why it happens and exactly how to fix it (hint: it's not just about glute strength)..."

üéØ Include Clear Calls-to-Action
Explicitly request specific engagement
Effective CTAs
  • "Comment 'SAVE' to bookmark this"
  • "Share this with someone who needs to hear it"
  • "What questions do you have about this technique?"
  • "Tag someone who always rushes their squats"

Clear CTAs significantly increase engagement likelihood.

#Ô∏è‚É£ Use Strategic Hashtags
Extend reach beyond existing followers

Research suggests 3-5 highly relevant hashtags perform better than excessive usage. Use specific rather than overly broad hashtags to reach targeted audiences more likely to engage.

‚ùå Overly Broad

#fitness #workout #training #exercise #health

(Reaches massive audiences with low relevance)

‚úì Targeted & Specific

(Smaller but more engaged audiences)

üìå Front-Load Important Information
Hook readers before caption truncation

Instagram truncates captions after 2-3 lines. Opening sentences should hook interest and communicate core value before users must click "more."

Structure Your Caption

Lines 1-3 (Pre-Truncation):

Hook with your main teaching point or question. Make readers want to click "more."

Lines 4+ (Post-Truncation):

Expand on the topic, provide additional context, include your CTA.

Post Timing Optimisation
‚ñº

Whilst quality outweighs timing, posting when target audiences are most active increases initial engagement likelihood, triggering positive algorithmic signals.

üìä Use Your Analytics
Research platform-specific audience activity patterns

Instagram and Facebook provide insights showing follower activity patterns by day and hour. Initial testing should explore different posting times, with ongoing strategy informed by performance data.

Action: Review your analytics monthly to identify when your specific followers are most active.

‚è∞ Typical Fitness Audience Activity Times
Peak Activity Windows

Early Morning (7-9am): Commute time, pre-workout planning

Lunch Break (12-1pm): Work break scrolling

Evening (5-7pm): Post-work wind down, commute home

Late Evening (8-10pm): Personal time

Weekend Mornings (8-11am): Weekly planning and weekend exercise

üí° Consider Less Competitive Times
Test posting when fewer accounts are active

Less competitive posting times (when fewer accounts post) may generate better engagement than peak times despite lower overall platform activity. Testing is essential for determining optimal timing for your specific audience.

Pro Tip: Compare engagement on posts made at peak times versus less competitive times. You may find your audience gives you better engagement when there's less competition for attention.
