Image Rights and Content Permissions

Essential legal and ethical considerations for using client images, music, and third-party content on social media

Using Client Images
‚ñº
Client images represent personal data and require informed consent before social media publication. Comprehensive image consent should address multiple considerations to protect both trainer and client interests.
Consent Must Address:
  • Whether face-obscuring is acceptable or if full facial images are permitted
  • Whether before-and-after transformation images may be shared
  • Whether images may be used in paid advertising campaigns
  • Which specific platforms will feature the images
  • Whether consent extends to the trainer's website and marketing materials beyond social media
‚úì Best Practices
Obtain written consent through dedicated forms rather than verbal agreements or message-based permissions. Consent forms should be signed before content creation rather than retrospectively, preventing situations where clients feel pressured to approve usage of content already created. Respect client preferences even when disappointed by usage limitations, recognising that clients may have professional, personal, or privacy concerns that justify restricted permissions.

Minors (individuals under 18) require parental or guardian consent for image publication. When working with younger clients, trainers must obtain consent from parents or legal guardians and consider whether the minor's image will remain appropriate for publication as they age. Particular sensitivity is required when posting images of adolescents in fitness environments.
Using Music in Social Media Content
‚ñº
Background music in social media videos raises copyright considerations. Understanding music licensing is essential to avoid content removal, account penalties, or legal action.
‚úì Safe Music Options
Platforms provide licensed music libraries that allow legal use within their services. Trainers should utilise these platform-provided options rather than incorporating copyrighted commercial music. Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook offer music libraries integrated into content creation tools, with licensing agreements allowing use within those specific platforms.
‚ö†Ô∏è Copyright Risks
Using copyrighted music without proper licensing risks content removal, account penalties, or legal action from rights holders. Even when content receives permission to remain on platforms temporarily, copyright claims can prevent monetisation or promotional boosting of content containing unauthorised music.

Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook all provide built-in music libraries specifically licensed for use on their platforms. These libraries are continuously updated with trending tracks and offer legal protection when used correctly within the platform's content creation tools.
Third-Party Content and Reposting
‚ñº
Sharing content created by others (such as client posts, educational infographics from other sources, or motivational content) requires appropriate attribution and, ideally, explicit permission.
‚ö†Ô∏è Platform Features ‚â† Automatic Permission
Whilst social media platforms provide built-in sharing mechanisms (such as Instagram story reposts or shared posts), these features do not automatically grant permission to use someone else's intellectual property for commercial purposes.
‚úì Best Practices for Sharing
Request permission before sharing others' content
Provide clear attribution crediting original creators
Use platform sharing features rather than downloading and re-uploading content
Verify permissions when sharing educational content from research organisations or fitness education providers
Provide proper citation for professional and academic sources
üìöEducational Content
When sharing educational content from research organisations, fitness education providers, or other professional sources, trainers should verify that the source permits sharing and provide proper citation. This demonstrates professionalism and protects against copyright infringement.
