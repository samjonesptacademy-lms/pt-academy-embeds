GDPR Principles Relevant to Social Media

Click each principle to explore its implications for fitness professionals using social media

Lawful Basis for Processing
▼
Personal data can only be collected and used when a lawful basis exists. For marketing purposes, this typically means obtaining explicit consent. When collecting email addresses through social media lead magnets, trainers must include clear consent mechanisms that explain exactly how the data will be used, obtain affirmative consent rather than pre-ticked boxes, and provide easy opt-out mechanisms.
Data Minimisation
▼
Collect only the personal data necessary for stated purposes. When running social media promotions or lead generation campaigns, request only essential information. For example, if sending a free training guide, only an email address is necessary; requesting extensive personal information without clear justification violates data minimisation principles.
Purpose Limitation
▼
Personal data collected for one purpose cannot be used for unrelated purposes without additional consent. Email addresses gathered for newsletter subscriptions cannot be used for promotional offers unless the original consent covered marketing communications, or additional permission has been obtained.
Right to Access and Erasure
▼
Individuals have rights to access their personal data and request deletion. Trainers must establish processes for handling these requests within legally required timeframes (typically 30 days). This includes deleting individuals from email marketing lists when requested and removing their information from client databases.
