Length
‚ñº
For fitness professionals with limited experience, CVs should typically be 1-2 pages. Recently qualified trainers can effectively present credentials on single pages, whilst experienced professionals with extensive relevant experience might extend to two pages.
CVs exceeding two pages risk losing employer attention unless extraordinary circumstances justify length.
üí° Quick Guide
New trainers: 1 page | Experienced professionals: 1-2 pages | Senior specialists: Maximum 2 pages

Font and Typography
‚ñº
Professional, readable fonts such as Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman in 10-12 point size ensure readability. Avoid decorative or highly stylised fonts appearing unprofessional or difficult to read.
Consistent font usage throughout maintains professional appearance.
‚úì Good Practice
Arial 11pt for body text, Arial 14pt bold for section headings

Comic Sans, cursive fonts, decorative scripts, or mixing multiple font families

White Space and Layout
‚ñº
Adequate white space prevents overwhelming, cramped appearance and aids readability. Appropriate margins (typically 2cm minimum), spacing between sections, and avoiding dense text blocks improve visual appeal and information processing.
üí° Layout Tips
Use consistent spacing: 1 line between items within sections, 1.5-2 lines between major sections. Don't fill every millimeter‚Äîwhite space is professional.

Common Formatting Mistakes
‚ñº
Several formatting choices significantly reduce CV effectiveness:
‚úó Avoid These Mistakes
‚Ä¢ Excessive colour or graphics (unless applying for creative visual roles)
‚Ä¢ Photos (UK CVs typically exclude photos unless specifically requested)
‚Ä¢ Dense, unbroken text blocks without adequate spacing or structure
‚Ä¢ Font size below 10pt or above 12pt for body text
‚Ä¢ Inconsistent or decorative bullet point styles
‚Ä¢ Tables or complex formatting that may not display correctly
üí° ATS Compatibility
Many employers use Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) scanning CVs for keywords before human review. Complex formatting, tables, graphics, or unusual layouts may not parse correctly through these systems, potentially resulting in qualified candidates being filtered out incorrectly. Simple, clean formatting ensures both human and system readability.
