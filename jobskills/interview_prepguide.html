üîç Employer Research ‚ñº

Understanding prospective employers demonstrates genuine interest and enables informed, relevant responses. Research helps you tailor answers and ask intelligent questions.

Key Research Areas:

  • Facility type and positioning (budget, premium, boutique, leisure centre)
  • Services offered and specialisations
  • Membership demographics and target markets
  • Values, mission, and culture
  • Recent news, developments, or changes
  • Key personnel including interviewers if known

Research Sources:

  • Employer websites and social media (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn)
  • Facility visits as members or guests where possible
  • Online reviews and member feedback (Google, Trustpilot)
  • Industry publications or news
  • Networking with current or former employees

Re-read job advertisements thoroughly before interviews. This preparation ensures you understand what employers are seeking and can demonstrate relevant experience.

What to Note from Job Ads:

  • Required and desirable qualifications and skills
  • Key responsibilities and duties
  • Specific competencies emphasised
  • Terminology used by the employer

Your Preparation:

Prepare specific examples from your experience demonstrating each requirement. Link your experience directly to what the job ad emphasises‚Äîthis shows you've understood their priorities.

Example:

If job ad requires: "Experience working with diverse client populations"

Prepare example: Describe specific client types you've worked with (beginners, older adults, post-injury clients) with concrete outcomes.

‚ùì Common Question Preparation ‚ñº

Whilst impossible to predict every interview question, certain questions appear frequently across fitness industry interviews. Preparing thoughtful responses builds confidence and ensures articulate delivery under pressure.

Common Interview Questions Include:

  • "Tell me about yourself"
  • "Why are you interested in this role/facility?"
  • "What are your strengths and weaknesses?"
  • "Tell me about a time you handled a difficult situation"
  • "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"
  • "How do you stay current with fitness industry trends?"
  • "Describe your approach to client programming"
  • "How do you handle feedback or criticism?"
üí° Pro Tip:
Practice responding to these questions aloud (not in your head). This develops fluency and confidence. Record yourself or practice with a friend for realistic feedback.
‚≠ê STAR Technique Mastery ‚ñº

The STAR technique (Situation, Task, Action, Result) provides structure for answering behavioural interview questions. This framework ensures comprehensive, organised responses demonstrating capabilities through concrete examples.

Situation: Briefly describe the context or challenge faced
Task: Explain what needed accomplishing or solving
Action: Detail specific steps taken or approaches used
Result: Describe outcomes achieved or lessons learned

Example Question: "Tell me about a time you dealt with a difficult client."

STAR Response:

Situation: "In my gym instructor role, I encountered a member who repeatedly used equipment unsafely despite previous gentle corrections."

Task: "I needed to address the behaviour firmly whilst maintaining a positive relationship."

Action: "I approached the member privately, explained the specific safety concerns, demonstrated proper technique, and offered to supervise their next few sessions to ensure they felt confident with correct form."

Result: "The member appreciated the private, supportive approach rather than public correction, improved their technique, and actually thanked me later for the help, becoming one of the gym's most engaged members."

This response demonstrates: communication skills, problem-solving ability, safety awareness, and positive outcome achievement through specific, concrete examples.

üé§ Questions to Ask Interviewers ‚ñº

Candidates should prepare thoughtful questions for interviewers demonstrating interest, engagement, and critical thinking. This also helps you evaluate if the role is right for you.

Appropriate Question Topics:

Role-Specific Details:

  • What does a typical day look like?
  • What are the immediate priorities for this role?
  • How is success measured in this position?
  • What training and support is provided?

Facility Operations:

  • What are the membership demographics?
  • How would you describe your facility culture?
  • What is the team structure and size?

Growth and Development:

  • What advancement opportunities exist?
  • How does the facility support CPD and specialist training?
  • Are there mentorship opportunities?

Practical Logistics:

  • What is the scheduling process?
  • How are clients allocated to trainers?
  • What administrative responsibilities are involved?
