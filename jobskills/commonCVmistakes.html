Spelling & Grammar
The Problem

Employment Gaps
The Problem
Significant unexplained gaps in employment history create concern and questions for employers. Gaps occur for legitimate reasons but addressing them prevents speculation.
‚úì Solution: Address gaps briefly and honestly. Examples: "Career break for family care" or "Travelled while completing fitness qualifications" provide context without extensive detail.

Generic Content
The Problem
Vague descriptions such as "good communicator," "hard worker," or "team player" without supporting evidence fail to differentiate candidates. These claims are applicable to any candidate.
‚úì Solution: Use specific examples, quantified achievements, and concrete skill demonstrations. Prove claims with evidence rather than making generic statements.

The Problem

Serious Violation
Dishonesty
The Problem
Fabricating qualifications, employment dates, or responsibilities represents serious ethical violations that can result in employment termination if discovered. Employers commonly verify qualifications and may contact previous employers.
‚úì Solution: Present experience positively and emphasise strengths, but never fabricate information. Discrepancies between CVs and reality will surface.
