5 April
Tax Year End
UK tax year ends. Record all income and expenses. Last chance for pension contributions.
The UK tax year runs from 6 April to 5 April. Ensure all income and expenses are recorded. This is your final opportunity to make pension contributions that count toward the current tax year's tax relief.

5 October
Registration Deadline
New self-employed MUST register with HMRC by this date. Late penalties apply.
If you became self-employed during the previous tax year (ended 5 April), you MUST register with HMRC by this date. Late registration attracts penalties even if you don't owe tax. Register at gov.uk/register-for-self-assessment.

31 October
Paper Returns
Deadline for paper Self Assessment. Most file online for extended deadline.
Deadline for paper Self Assessment tax returns (if not filing online). Most self-employed trainers file online for the extended deadline of 31 January. Paper filing is rarely used but available if needed.

31 January
Payment & Filing
CRITICAL: Online return AND payment due. Includes balance + first payment on account. ¬£100+ penalties if missed.
Online Self Assessment tax return must be submitted AND payment made by midnight. This includes: (1) Any balance owed for previous tax year, (2) First payment on account for current tax year. Missing this deadline results in automatic ¬£100 penalty plus daily penalties and interest.

31 July
Second Payment
Second payment on account due. Typically half your previous year's tax bill.
Second payment on account due for current tax year. This is typically half your previous year's tax bill. If your income has decreased significantly, you can reduce payments on account, but you'll owe the difference if you underestimate.

Ongoing
Record Keeping
Maintain continuous records. Keep receipts, invoices, bank statements for 5+ years.
Maintain continuous records of all income and expenses. Keep receipts, invoices, and bank statements. Records must be retained for at least 5 years after the 31 January submission deadline. Good ongoing bookkeeping prevents year-end panic.

Best Practice
Early Deadlines
Submit by 31 Dec. Set aside tax monthly (25-35% of profit). Gives buffer for issues.
Don't wait until official deadlines. Aim to submit Self Assessment by 31 December and set aside tax funds monthly (25-35% of profit). This gives buffer for technical issues and prevents cash flow problems when payments fall due.
