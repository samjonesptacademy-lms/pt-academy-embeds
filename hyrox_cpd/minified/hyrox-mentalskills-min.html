Fatigue Management and Pacing Education

Many faults emerge from poor fatigue management and pacing causing premature breakdown

Many technical faults emerge not from lack of skill but from poor fatigue management and pacing strategies that cause premature breakdown. Effective coaching addresses both the physical and strategic elements of endurance performance.
1
Teaching Sustainable Pacing
▼
Clients often attempt unsustainable intensity creating dramatic performance decline. Education focuses on demonstrating the long-term benefits of controlled pace versus aggressive-then-collapse approaches.
Key Education Strategies
Demonstrating long-term benefits of controlled pace versus aggressive-then-collapse approaches
Using RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion) scales teaching intensity management
Practicing target paces to become familiar with sustainable output
Comparing finish times between controlled and aggressive pacing strategies
2
Strategic Rest
▼
Teaching when and how to rest optimizes performance. Strategic rest prevents complete exhaustion and maintains overall work output.
Strategic Rest Approaches
Planned brief rests at predetermined points versus unplanned stops from exhaustion
Accepting slight pace reduction while maintaining continuous movement versus frequent complete stops
Breathing-focused mini-breaks (3-5 breaths) versus extended rest periods
Station-specific rest strategies based on grip, breathing, or muscular demands
3
Pre-Fatigue Training
▼
Practicing movements under fatigue identifies breakdown points and develops tolerance for maintaining technique when tired. This prepares athletes for race-specific demands.
Pre-Fatigue Methods
Completing stations after significant running volume
Performing multiple stations consecutively with minimal rest
High-volume station practice building fatigue tolerance
Race simulation sessions identifying specific breakdown points
Progressive overload in fatigue conditions
