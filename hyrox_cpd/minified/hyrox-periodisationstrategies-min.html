Periodisation Strategies

Systematic periodisation organizes training into phases emphasizing different qualities, enabling peak performance whilst preventing overtraining

1
Base Phase
4-6 Weeks
Focus:
Aerobic base, general strength, movement technique
Volume/Intensity:
High volume, Low-moderate intensity
Emphasis:
Building capacity for subsequent phases
2
Build Phase
4-6 Weeks
Focus:
HYROX-specific work, race simulations
Volume/Intensity:
Moderate-high volume, Moderate-high intensity
Emphasis:
Event-specific preparation and performance development
3
Peak/Taper
1-2 Weeks
Focus:
Reducing volume, maintaining intensity
Volume/Intensity:
Reduced 40-60%, Intensity maintained
Emphasis:
Arriving rested and prepared
Sample Weekly Microcycle (Build Phase)
Weekly structure strategically sequencing session types for optimal adaptation and recovery.
Monday
Easy run 45 minutes + strength work
Tuesday
HYROX interval session (run + stations)
Wednesday
Recovery (easy 30 minutes or rest)
Thursday
Tempo run 30 minutes + station work
Friday
Strength and conditioning
Saturday
Long run 75 minutes OR partial simulation
Sunday
Complete rest or active recovery
Key Microcycle Patterns
• Hard running followed by easy days or strength focus
• Alternating high-intensity with recovery sessions
• Strength training after running (preserving running quality)
• 1-2 complete rest or active recovery days weekly
