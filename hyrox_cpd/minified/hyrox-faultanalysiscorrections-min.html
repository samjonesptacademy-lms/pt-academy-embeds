Fault Analysis & Station Corrections

Systematic framework for identifying and correcting technical faults

Systematic Fault Analysis Framework

1
Observation Phase
Careful observation noting specific deviations, identifying when faults occur (immediately, under fatigue, at specific ranges), assessing consistency (every rep, intermittent), and observing compensatory patterns suggesting underlying causes.
2
Diagnosis Phase
Determining fault root causes: mobility/flexibility limitations, strength or stability deficits, motor control or coordination issues, fatigue-related breakdowns, or conceptual misunderstanding. Accurate diagnosis determines appropriate correction approach.
3
Intervention Phase
Implementing targeted corrections: mobility work addressing flexibility limitations, strength training targeting weak groups, motor control drills improving coordination, regression to simpler variations establishing patterns, or education clarifying requirements.
