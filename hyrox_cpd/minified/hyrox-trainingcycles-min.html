Early Cycle
Base Building Phase
The early cycle focuses on building foundational fitness and establishing movement patterns without the pressure of high intensity or specificity. This phase creates the aerobic base and general strength that supports all subsequent training.
Key Priorities
• High volume, lower intensity training
• Aerobic base development through easy running
• General strength and movement technique
• Building training tolerance and work capacity
2
Mid Cycle
2
Mid Cycle
Specific Development Phase
The mid cycle transitions toward HYROX-specific training whilst maintaining the aerobic foundation built earlier. Intensity increases and training becomes more specific to race demands, including regular practice of all stations and race simulations.
Key Priorities
• Moderate-high volume with increasing intensity
• HYROX-specific station work and transitions
• Race pace running intervals
• Partial race simulations and combination workouts
3
Late Cycle
3
Late Cycle
Peak & Taper Phase
The late cycle reduces training volume whilst maintaining intensity, allowing accumulated fatigue to dissipate so athletes arrive at competition rested and prepared. Training becomes highly specific with shorter, race-intensity sessions.
Key Priorities
• Reduced volume (40-60% decrease)
• Maintained or slightly increased intensity
• Short, sharp race-specific sessions
• Recovery prioritization and race preparation
