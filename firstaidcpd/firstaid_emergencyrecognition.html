Cardiovascular Emergency
Cardiac Arrest
Recognition Signs
  • Sudden collapse and loss of consciousness
  • Unresponsive to verbal or physical stimuli
  • Absent or abnormal breathing (gasping)
  • No signs of circulation
Appropriate Response
Alert designated first aiders and call 999 immediately. If trained personnel and AED available, CPR and defibrillation required. Untrained persons should follow call handler instructions.

Cardiovascular Emergency
Heart Attack
Recognition Signs
  • Central chest pain, pressure, or tightness
  • Pain radiating to arm, jaw, neck, or back
  • Shortness of breath
  • Nausea, sweating, pale/clammy skin
  • Sense of impending doom
Appropriate Response
Call 999 immediately. Help person into comfortable position (usually sitting). If they have prescribed nitroglycerin, help them access it. Monitor and reassure until help arrives.

Respiratory Emergency
Asthma Attack
Recognition Signs
  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Wheezing sound during breathing
  • Coughing, chest tightness
  • Inability to complete sentences
  • Distress and anxiety
Appropriate Response
Help person sit upright and use their inhaler if available. Encourage slow, calm breathing. Call 999 if severe, not improving, or no inhaler available. Monitor closely.

Metabolic Emergency
Hypoglycemia (Low Blood Sugar)
Recognition Signs
  • Sudden weakness, shakiness, trembling
  • Sweating despite cool temperature
  • Irritability, confusion, mood changes
  • Dizziness, rapid heartbeat
  • Severe cases: loss of consciousness
Appropriate Response
If conscious, help person access their glucose tablets, juice, or sugary food. Monitor improvement. Call 999 if unconscious, not improving, or seizures occur.

Neurological Emergency
Seizure
Recognition Signs
  • Sudden loss of consciousness and falling
  • Rhythmic jerking movements of limbs
  • Rigid muscles or stiffening
  • Loss of bladder/bowel control
  • Confusion or disorientation afterward
Appropriate Response
Protect from injury (move hazards away), cushion head, time the seizure. Do NOT restrain. Call 999 if seizure lasts >5 minutes, repeated seizures, or first-time occurrence.

Trauma Emergency
Severe Bleeding
Recognition Signs
  • Blood spurting from wound
  • Bleeding not slowing with pressure
  • Blood soaking through dressings
  • Large or deep wounds
  • Signs of shock developing
Appropriate Response
Alert first aiders and call 999. First aid trained responders will apply direct pressure, elevate if possible. Use universal precautions. Monitor for shock.

Environmental Emergency
Heat Stroke
Recognition Signs
  • Core temperature above 40¬∞C
  • Altered mental state (confusion, aggression)
  • Hot, dry skin (sweating may cease)
  • Rapid, strong pulse
  • Possible seizures or loss of consciousness
Appropriate Response
Call 999 immediately. Move to cool environment. First aid trained responders will initiate cooling measures. This is life-threatening and requires emergency medical care.

Circulation Issue
Fainting (Syncope)
Recognition Signs
  • Brief loss of consciousness (seconds to minutes)
  • Preceding dizziness, light-headedness, nausea
  • Pale appearance, sweating
  • Usually regains consciousness when lying flat
  • May feel weak afterward
Appropriate Response
Help person lie flat with legs elevated. Ensure airway is clear. Monitor recovery. Call 999 if prolonged unconsciousness, chest pain, fainting during exercise, or first occurrence.
