üö® Emergency Identification
Defines what constitutes emergencies requiring plan activation. Categorizes by severity (minor incidents vs. major emergencies) and type (medical, fire, security, structural).

Clearly assigns who assesses situations, provides direct care, contacts emergency services, manages bystanders, documents incidents, and communicates with management.

‚ö†Ô∏è Why EAP Familiarity Matters
All fitness staff must be thoroughly familiar with facility EAPs through regular training, accessible written copies, periodic drills, and clear understanding of personal roles. During actual emergencies, stress and urgency impair decision-making and memory. Familiar, well-rehearsed protocols enable effective action despite cognitive challenges. Unfamiliarity results in confusion, delays, and potentially harmful improvisation.

Example emergency action plan for a typical gym environment

Emergency Classification
Major Emergencies (Activate Full EAP):
  • Cardiac arrest or no breathing
  • Severe bleeding or traumatic injury
  • Loss of consciousness
  • Suspected spinal injury
  • Fire or structural damage
  • Violent or threatening situation
Minor Incidents (First Aid Response):
  • Minor cuts, scrapes, bruises
  • Muscle strains or minor sprains
  • Nosebleeds
  • Minor equipment malfunction
üë•
Assigned Roles
First Responder (First qualified staff member on scene): Assesses situation, provides immediate care, directs others

Emergency Caller (Designated by First Responder): Calls 999, provides clear information, remains on line, meets emergency services at entrance

Crowd Control (Senior staff member): Manages bystanders, maintains clear area, prevents video recording, maintains privacy

Equipment Retrieval (Available staff): Brings first aid kit, AED, emergency equipment as directed

Documentation (Duty Manager): Completes incident report, notifies management, secures witness statements

Emergency Contact Procedure
Calling 999:
  1. State: "Emergency at [Facility Name], [Full Address], [Postcode]"
  2. Describe emergency type and number of casualties
  3. Provide specific location within facility
  4. Follow dispatcher instructions
  5. Do NOT hang up until told to do so
  6. Send staff member to meet ambulance at main entrance
Emergency Phone Locations: Reception desk, gym floor office, studio control room. Facility Address Card posted at each location.
üè•
Emergency Equipment Locations
  • First Aid Kits: Reception, gym floor (near weights area), studio changing rooms, pool area
  • AED (Defibrillator): Main reception wall (clearly marked), gym floor emergency station
  • Emergency Phones: Reception, gym floor office, studio control
  • Emergency Exits: Main entrance, rear fire exit (near studios), pool area emergency exit, car park exit
  • Assembly Point: Car park far corner, away from building entrance
All equipment checked weekly. AED pads expire: [DATE]. Next inspection: [DATE].

Cardiac Arrest Protocol
  1. Check for danger, responsiveness, breathing
  2. Shout for help - activate EAP immediately
  3. Designate someone to call 999 and retrieve AED
  4. Begin CPR: 30 compressions, 2 breaths
  5. Apply AED as soon as available - follow voice prompts
  6. Continue until emergency services arrive or person recovers
  7. Rotate CPR providers every 2 minutes if possible
DO NOT stop CPR to move the person unless in immediate danger. Quality compressions save lives.
üî•
Fire Evacuation Protocol
  1. Sound fire alarm immediately upon discovering fire
  2. Do NOT attempt to fight fire - evacuate immediately
  3. Staff close doors (do not lock), guide members to nearest exit
  4. Check toilets, changing rooms, studios for remaining persons
  5. Proceed to assembly point calmly but quickly
  6. Duty Manager conducts headcount using sign-in system
  7. Await fire service - report missing persons immediately
  8. Do NOT re-enter building until fire service gives all-clear

Incident Documentation
Complete within 24 hours of incident:
  • Incident Report Form (located in duty manager office)
  • Date, time, location of incident
  • Names of casualty, witnesses, staff involved
  • Detailed description of what occurred
  • Actions taken and by whom
  • Emergency services attendance and advice given
  • Outcome and follow-up required
Reports stored in locked filing cabinet (Duty Manager office). Notify Regional Manager within 4 hours of major emergencies.
