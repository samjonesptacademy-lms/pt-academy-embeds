Understanding roles, responsibilities, and how to work effectively together in emergency situations

Supporting First Aiders Effectively
â–¼

When first aiders respond to emergencies, your support is crucial:

  • Provide clear, concise information about what happened and what you've observed
  • Follow their instructions and requests for assistance
  • Avoid interfering with assessment or treatment
  • Manage bystanders to give first aiders space to work
  • Retrieve requested equipment quickly
  • Take notes if asked, to assist with documentation
  • Remain available in case additional help is needed

Pro Tip: First aiders work best when they can focus entirely on the casualty. Give them space, provide information when asked, and handle scene management tasks.

Conflicts or Concerns
â–¼

If you believe first aiders are acting inappropriately or missing critical information:

  • Politely share your concerns: "I noticed she mentioned chest pain earlier, I'm not sure if you heard that"
  • Provide additional information if asked
  • If genuinely concerned about safety, you can independently call emergency services (which first aiders should support rather than resist)
  • Raise concerns with management after incidents resolve, if appropriate

Important: First aiders have authority in emergency situations, but this doesn't prevent others from expressing genuine safety concerns or providing additional relevant information. Patient safety is the priority.
