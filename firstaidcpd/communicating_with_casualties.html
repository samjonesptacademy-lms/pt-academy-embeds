Communication & reassurance

Use calm, clear communication to reduce anxiety and support the casualty while help is on the way.

Displaying calm confidence helps reduce casualty anxiety. Even when feeling uncertain internally, projecting calm competence benefits casualties psychologically.

Briefly state your name and role: “I’m Alex, I work here. Help is on the way.”

Describe what you’re doing and what will happen: “An ambulance is coming,” “We’re just going to help you lie down,” “The first aider will be here in a moment.”

Reassure casualties they’re being helped without making specific promises about outcomes: “You’re in good hands,” “Help will be here very soon,” rather than “You’ll be fine” or “Everything will be okay” which may be untrue.

If casualties can communicate, listen to their concerns, questions, or information about their condition. Individuals with chronic conditions often know what’s wrong and what helps them.

Protect casualty privacy and dignity during assessment and treatment, particularly regarding clothing, positioning, or personal information.

Provide updates periodically: “The ambulance should be here any minute,” “The first aider is just getting equipment now.”
