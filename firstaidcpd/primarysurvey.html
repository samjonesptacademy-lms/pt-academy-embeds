Primary Survey: ABC Approach

A rapid assessment to identify immediate life threats

The primary survey identifies conditions requiring immediate intervention. Once the scene is safe, assess casualties using the ABC approach to detect life-threatening problems with Airway, Breathing, and Circulation.

A
Airway
Is the airway clear and open?
▼
What to Assess
Check whether the casualty's airway is clear and open (patent). A blocked or compromised airway prevents breathing and is immediately life-threatening.
Signs of Airway Problems
Noisy breathing (snoring, gurgling sounds)
Inability to speak or difficulty speaking
Visible obstruction in mouth
Complete silence when casualty appears to be trying to breathe
Awareness-Level Response
✓ Your Actions (Without Training)
• Immediately alert designated first aiders
• Call 999 if airway is compromised
• Do NOT attempt to clear airway yourself
• Do NOT move the casualty unless in immediate danger
• Monitor and be ready to provide information to responders
⚠️ Important
Airway management requires specific training. Do not attempt to open airways or remove obstructions without proper first aid qualification.
B
Breathing
Is the person breathing normally?
▼
What to Assess
Determine whether the casualty is breathing normally. For unconscious casualties, look for chest movement, listen for breath sounds, and watch for signs of breathing.
Signs of Abnormal Breathing
No breathing at all
Gasping or agonal breathing (irregular gasps)
Very rapid or very slow breathing
Extreme difficulty breathing despite being conscious
Blue-tinged lips or skin (cyanosis)
Awareness-Level Response
✓ Your Actions (Without Training)
• If not breathing: Call 999 IMMEDIATELY and alert first aiders
• Follow emergency dispatcher instructions
• If breathing but unconscious: Monitor closely and call 999
• If difficulty breathing: Help person into comfortable position, call 999
• Do NOT attempt rescue breathing or CPR without training
⚠️ Critical
Absence of normal breathing is a life-threatening emergency. Call 999 immediately. Trained first aiders will provide CPR if needed.
C
Circulation
Is circulation adequate?
▼
What to Assess
For untrained persons, circulation assessment focuses on observing for signs of adequate blood flow rather than checking pulses (which is unreliable without training). Look for signs of life and circulation problems.
Signs of Circulation Problems
Pale, cold, clammy skin
Blue-tinged lips or nail beds
Absence of signs of life (no movement, no normal breathing)
Severe bleeding (blood spurting or heavy flow)
Deteriorating consciousness level
Awareness-Level Response
✓ Your Actions (Without Training)
• Severe bleeding: Alert first aiders immediately, call 999
• Do NOT check for pulse (unreliable without training)
• Look for signs of life: movement, normal breathing, responsiveness
• If no signs of life: Call 999, alert first aiders for CPR
• Monitor skin color and warmth as indicators of shock
⚠️ Remember
Severe bleeding requires immediate intervention by trained first aiders. Do not attempt direct pressure without gloves and training. Absence of signs of life requires immediate CPR by trained personnel.
