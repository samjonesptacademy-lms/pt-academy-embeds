Components of Duty of Care

Understanding the three key components that define a fitness professional's duty of care. Click each section to expand.

1
Competence
▼

Fitness professionals must maintain competence appropriate to their roles through proper qualifications, continuing professional development, working within scope of practice, and maintaining professional registrations where required.

2
Reasonable Actions
▼

Duty of care requires taking actions that reasonable, competent professionals would take in similar circumstances. This includes recognising emergencies requiring intervention, following established protocols appropriately, seeking help when situations exceed capabilities, and avoiding actions that could worsen conditions.

3
Foreseeability
▼

Professionals must anticipate and prepare for reasonably foreseeable risks. In fitness environments, certain emergencies are foreseeable including exercise-induced cardiac events, musculoskeletal injuries, hypoglycemia in diabetic clients, asthma attacks, and slips, trips, and falls.
