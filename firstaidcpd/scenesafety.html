Danger Assessment
‚ñº
Before approaching casualties, quickly assess for:
  • Ongoing hazards (falling weights, unstable equipment, wet floors, electrical hazards)
  • Risk of injury to responders or bystanders
  • Whether scene can be made safe or requires professional emergency services before approach
If scenes cannot be made safe, the appropriate action is maintaining safe distance, preventing others from entering danger zones, and immediately contacting emergency services. Becoming additional casualties serves no purpose and compounds emergencies.

Making Scenes Safe
‚ñº
When possible, make scenes safe by:
  • Switching off dangerous equipment
  • Clearing surrounding hazards
  • Controlling bystanders to maintain space
  • Ensuring adequate lighting for assessment and treatment
However, do not place yourself at risk attempting to make scenes safe if doing so requires specialised knowledge or creates danger.

Responder Safety First
‚ñº
Your safety is the priority. A safe responder can help; an injured responder becomes part of the problem. Always:
  • Stop and assess before rushing to help
  • Never enter scenes you cannot safely exit
  • Call for professional help when hazards exceed your capability
  • Monitor the scene continuously - conditions can change
Scenario 1 of 4
Collapsed Member Near Treadmill
You hear a thud and see a member collapsed on the floor next to a treadmill. The treadmill is still running at high speed. The member is not moving.
What should you do first?
Immediately rush over and check if the person is breathing
Shout for help while running towards the casualty
Stop the treadmill using the emergency stop, then approach the casualty
Call 999 immediately before approaching
Scenario 2 of 4
Weight Rack Collapse
A storage rack holding dumbbells has partially collapsed. A member is trapped underneath with weights on their legs. The rack is unstable and more weights could fall.
What is the safest immediate action?
Immediately try to lift the weights off the casualty
Stabilize the rack if possible, call for additional help, then carefully remove weights
Pull the person out from under the rack quickly
Wait for emergency services before taking any action
Scenario 3 of 4
Wet Floor Emergency
A member has slipped on a wet floor near the pool area and appears to be injured. The floor is still very wet and slippery. Other members are nearby and could also slip.
What should you prioritize?
Carefully walk across the wet floor to reach the casualty
Alert others to the hazard, find a safe approach path, then reach the casualty
Start mopping the floor before helping the casualty
Ask other members to help you reach the casualty
Scenario 4 of 4
Electrical Equipment Fault
A member is lying unconscious next to a piece of electronic equipment that is sparking and smoking. You can smell burning.
What is the correct response?
Quickly pull the person away from the equipment
Pour water on the equipment to stop the sparking
Cut power at the mains/isolate the circuit, evacuate the area, call emergency services
Use a fire extinguisher on the equipment first
