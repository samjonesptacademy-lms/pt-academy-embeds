Scope of Practice & Professional Boundaries

Understanding what you can and should do, what requires specific training, and when to seek help

✅
What You CAN and SHOULD Do
▼
These actions are appropriate and expected for fitness professionals without regulated first aid qualifications. You can provide valuable assistance within these boundaries.
✓
Recognise signs of medical emergencies
✓
Follow facility emergency action plans
✓
Alert designated first aiders
✓
Call emergency services when needed
✓
Provide clear information using frameworks like LIONEL
✓
Manage bystanders and maintain scene safety
✓
Offer comfort and reassurance to casualties
✓
Document incidents appropriately
✓
Help casualties access their own prescribed medications (inhalers, glucose tablets)
❌
What You SHOULD NOT Do Without Specific Training
▼
These actions require regulated first aid training and qualifications. Attempting them without proper training could cause harm and create legal liability.
✗
Provide first aid treatments (CPR, bandaging, splinting)
✗
Use AEDs without training
✗
Move casualties with suspected spinal injuries
✗
Make medical diagnoses
✗
Decide whether emergency services are "really needed"
✗
Prescribe or administer medications (except helping with casualties' own medications)
✗
Attempt interventions beyond your training level
⚠️
When Uncertain
▼
When in doubt, these principles will keep you and the casualty safe. It's always better to seek help than to attempt something beyond your capability.
▲
Err on the side of caution
▲
Ask for help from designated first aiders
▲
Call emergency services if seriously concerned
▲
Follow dispatcher instructions carefully
▲
Let designated first aiders or medical professionals take charge
▲
Remember: unnecessary ambulance calls are preferable to delayed help for serious conditions
